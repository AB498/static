Citizen’s Advice

A UK-wide debt charity offering free support and money management guidance, available via a free helpline number.

The Money Advice Service

Web: moneyadviceservice.org.uk

On hand with free and impartial money advice from the UK government.

For help with your mental wellbeing.

Anxiety UK

Web: anxietyuk.org.uk
Phone: 03444 775 774
Open: Mon-Fri, 9:30am-7:30pm; Sat-Sun, 10am-2pm

A national charity that can offer support for anyone who’s been diagnosed with an anxiety condition. Offers community support, therapists and resources to help you.

MIND

Web: mind.org.uk

Resources for getting help with mental health problems, including help for those in urgent need and a resource bank of other organisations who can help.

The Samaritans

Web: samaritans.org.uk
Phone: 116 123
Open: 24-hours a day

A UK-wide charity offering confidential support to help people who are struggling with feelings of distress or despair.

For help with a gambling problem.

Gambler’s Anonymous

Web: gamblersanonymous.org.uk

Online support for compulsive gamblers, including a forum, resources, chat room and meeting finder.

National Gambling Helpline

Web: gamblersanonymous.org.uk
Phone: 0808 8020 133
Open: 8am-midnight

A 24-hour freephone number offering information, advice and support, in confidence, for anyone affected by gambling problems in Scotland, England and Wales.

For help with addiction.

Alcoholics Anonymous

Web: alcoholics-anonymous.org.uk
Phone: 0800 917 7650
Open: 24-hours a day

An anonymous support network founded in 1935 for people who want to stop drinking and find support to live a new life without alcohol.

Narcotics Anonymous

Web: ukna.org
Phone: 0300 999 1212
Open: Daily, 10am-midnight

Offering ways to live life without drugs and peer support via meetings.

Your debt help application will be processed by one of our debt advisors. These in-house advisors will go through all your possible options, including IVA (Individual Voluntary Arrangement), DRO (Debt Relief Order) and DMP (Debt Management Plan), which are available for people living in England, Wales and Northern Ireland. Help can only be offered following an initial fact-finding process.
